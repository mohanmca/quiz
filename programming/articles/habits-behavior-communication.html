1) Foundations: Habit, Behavior, Character

  • Habit Loop: Cue → Routine → Reward (keep the cue, swap the routine, keep/adjust the reward).
  • B=MAP (Fogg): Behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and Prompt coincide. Lower friction first.
  • Identity-Based Habits: “Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become.”
  • Character: Accumulated consequence of your repeated decisions and behaviors, especially when unobserved.
“We are what we repeatedly do; excellence, then, is not an act but a habit.” — often attributed to Aristotle

5) Discarding Bad Habits

  • Audit: List daily routines. Mark + (helps), – (hurts), ~ (neutral).
  • Remove Cues: Out of sight: uninstall apps, grayscale phone, hide snacks, charge phone away from bed.
  • Increase Friction: Add steps before the bad habit (logins, blockers, time locks, accountability).
  • Swap Routine: Keep cue, insert a healthier routine, keep a similar reward (e.g., walk + water for energy).
  • Never Miss Twice: Accept slips, reset next action immediately.

6) Cultivating Good Habits

  • Make it Obvious: Prepare environment: put book on pillow, guitar on stand, shoes at door.
  • Make it Tiny: 2‑minute version to start (one push-up, one page, one sentence).
  • Make it Attractive: Pair with music, tea, or a positive ritual; use habit stacking.
  • Make it Satisfying: Visual streaks, checkmarks, small immediate rewards.
  • Weekly Review: Promote what worked, prune what didn’t; iterate one variable at a time.

8) Nonviolent Communication (NVC) & Empathy

NVC focuses on needs and collaborative problem solving. Pair it with active, empathic listening.

  • Four Components: Observation (without judgment) → Feeling → Need → Request (clear, doable, present-focused).
  • Empathic Listening: Attend fully; paraphrase content; reflect feelings; ask open questions; withhold fixing/defending.
  • Sample Scripts:
    • Observation: "In our last two sprints, the report was late by a day."
    • Feeling/Need: "I feel stressed because I need reliability."
    • Request: "Could we add a mid-sprint checkpoint and a 24h reminder?"
  • Boundaries with Care: "I don’t take meetings past 6pm; I’m available 9–5. Would 10am tomorrow work?"
  • Empathy Blockers to Avoid: moralizing, unsolicited advice, rebuttal listening, story hijacking, diagnosing.

9) Goal Achievement: Science Tips & Tricks

  • Implementation Intentions: If [cue], then I will [specific action]. Example: If it’s 7am and I finish coffee, I will read one page.
  • WOOP (Oettingen): Wish → Outcome → Obstacle → Plan (If obstacle, then response). Use mental contrasting to surface real blockers.
  • Precommitment: Create constraints that make the right action easy and the wrong action hard (blockers, social commitments, deposits).
  • Temptation Bundling: Pair a should-do with a want-to (gym only with your favorite podcast).
  • Fresh Start Effect: Use temporal landmarks (Mondays, birthdays) to reset identity and routines; schedule starts on these days.
  • Planning Fallacy Antidotes: Break into sub-steps with DoD; add 30–50% time buffer; reference class-based estimates.
  • Goal Shielding: Dedicate distraction-free blocks; silence notifications; prepare environment the night before.

11) Scenarios & Model Phrasing

  • Missed deadline: “Yesterday’s deliverable slipped. What blocked us? Let’s agree on two earlier checkpoints next time.”
  • Late-night phone: Charge phone outside bedroom; read 1 page in bed.
  • 3pm slump: Replace snack+scroll with walk+water+protein.
  • Disagreement: “I see it differently because … What would a good outcome look like for you?”
